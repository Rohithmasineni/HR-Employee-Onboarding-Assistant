LEAVE POLICY — COMPREHENSIVE HR GUIDE
=======================================
Company: TechCorp Solutions Pvt. Ltd.
Effective From: January 1, 2024
Last Updated: April 2025
Document Owner: HR Department

SECTION 1: LEAVE TYPES & ENTITLEMENTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1.1 Casual Leave (CL)
----------------------
- Entitlement     : 12 days per calendar year
- Purpose         : Personal errands, family events, short personal work
- Max consecutive : 3 days at a time (beyond that, use Earned Leave)
- Advance notice  : Minimum 2 working days in advance
- Can be taken    : Half-day basis allowed (0.5 day)
- Carry forward   : NOT allowed. Lapses on December 31st each year
- During probation: Included in the 5-day probation limit

1.2 Sick Leave (SL)
--------------------
- Entitlement     : 10 days per calendar year
- Purpose         : Personal illness, medical appointments, health recovery
- Medical proof   : Required if availed for more than 2 consecutive days
                    (Submit medical certificate within 3 days of rejoining)
- Advance notice  : Not required — can be applied on the same day
- Can be taken    : Half-day basis allowed
- Carry forward   : NOT allowed. Lapses on December 31st each year
- During probation: Included in the 5-day probation limit
- Note            : Sick leave cannot be combined with casual leave for extended absences

1.3 Earned Leave (EL) / Privilege Leave (PL)
---------------------------------------------
- Entitlement     : 15 days per calendar year
- Purpose         : Planned vacations, family trips, extended personal time
- Advance notice  : Minimum 7 working days in advance
- Min duration    : At least 2 consecutive working days
- Can be taken    : NOT on half-day basis
- Carry forward   : Up to 30 days can be carried forward to next year (with HR approval)
- Encashment      : Unused EL beyond carry-forward limit can be encashed at year-end
- During probation: NOT available during probation period
- Recommended for : Long leaves of 5+ days

1.4 Maternity Leave (ML)
--------------------------
- Entitlement     : 26 weeks (as per Maternity Benefit Act, 1961)
- Eligibility     : Female employees who have worked for at least 80 days in the last 12 months
- Paid/Unpaid     : Fully paid
- Documentation   : Medical certificate from a registered gynecologist required
- Apply at least  : 8 weeks before expected delivery date
- Miscarriage     : 6 weeks of paid leave applicable
- Adoption        : 12 weeks for mothers adopting a child below 3 months of age
- Contact         : hr@company.com for maternity leave queries

1.5 Paternity Leave (PL)
--------------------------
- Entitlement     : 5 days
- Eligibility     : Male employees on birth of a child
- Must be taken   : Within 15 days of the child's birth
- Documentation   : Birth certificate of the child (submit within 30 days)

1.6 Bereavement Leave
----------------------
- Entitlement     : 3 days for immediate family (spouse, children, parents, siblings)
                    1 day for extended family (grandparents, in-laws)
- Paid/Unpaid     : Fully paid
- Documentation   : Death certificate (submit within 1 week of rejoining)
- Additional leave: If travel beyond 300 km is required, 2 extra days may be granted by HR

1.7 Marriage Leave
-------------------
- Entitlement     : 3 days (one-time, for own marriage)
- Notice required : Minimum 2 weeks in advance
- Documentation   : Marriage invitation or certificate

1.8 Compensatory Off (Comp Off)
--------------------------------
- Applicable when : Employee works on a declared holiday or weekend due to business need
- Must be approved: Manager approval required before working on holiday
- Validity        : Comp off must be availed within 60 days of the working holiday
- Process         : Submit comp-off request through HR portal after manager approves

1.9 Loss of Pay (LOP)
----------------------
- When applicable : When all paid leave balances are exhausted and absence is unapproved
- Impact          : Deducted directly from monthly salary
- Unauthorized LOP: More than 3 consecutive LOP days without notice may lead to disciplinary action

SECTION 2: GENERAL RULES & GUIDELINES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

2.1 Basic Rules
----------------
1. Leave request must be applied at least 2 days in advance (except Sick Leave).
2. Sick Leave can be applied on the same day via HR portal or by informing the manager.
3. Leaves cannot be carried forward to the next calendar year (except Earned Leave up to 30 days).
4. Leave during probation period is limited to 5 days total (CL + SL combined).
5. Unapproved absence will be treated as Loss of Pay (LOP).
6. Leaves cannot be sandwiched around public holidays to extend the break without approval.
7. Employees must handover pending work to a colleague before going on leave.
8. Leaves during notice period are generally not approved unless critical.

2.2 Public Holidays
--------------------
- The company observes approximately 10 national and regional public holidays per year.
- The holiday list is published in January each year on the HR portal.
- If an employee is required to work on a public holiday, they are eligible for Comp Off.

2.3 Leave During Notice Period
--------------------------------
- Earned Leave applications during notice period will not be approved in most cases.
- Casual or Sick Leave may be considered on a case-by-case basis by HR.
- Unused leave balances will NOT be encashed automatically upon resignation — only EL can be encashed as per policy.

SECTION 3: LEAVE APPLICATION PROCESS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Step 1: Log in to the HR portal (portal.techcorp.com)
Step 2: Navigate to "My Leaves" → "Apply Leave"
Step 3: Select leave type, start date, end date, and reason
Step 4: Submit — your Reporting Manager receives an email approval request
Step 5: You will be notified via email (approved/rejected within 1 working day)
Step 6: For Sick Leave, upload medical certificate if leave exceeded 2 days

EMERGENCY LEAVE:
- Inform your manager via phone/WhatsApp immediately
- Apply formally on the HR portal the same day or next working day

SECTION 4: LEAVE BALANCE & TRACKING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

- Leave balances reset on January 1st every year
- Employees can check their real-time balance on the HR portal under "My Leaves"
- Leave history (approved, rejected, pending) is visible in the portal
- Monthly payslips reflect leaves taken and any LOP deductions
- At year-end (December), HR sends a leave balance summary to all employees
